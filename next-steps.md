# PocketSod — Next Steps

## Immediate (before actively marketing)

**1. Wire up the contact form**
Right now the form doesn't actually send anywhere — it just fakes a success message. Set up [Formspree](https://formspree.io) (free, takes 5 minutes) so leads actually reach your inbox. Claude can do this.

**2. Submit to Google Search Console**
Go to [search.google.com/search-console](https://search.google.com/search-console), verify ownership of `www.pocketsod.com`, then submit `https://www.pocketsod.com/sitemap.xml`. This tells Google to actively index you. Requires your Google account — do this yourself.

**3. Set up Google Business Profile**
Free listing at [business.google.com](https://business.google.com). Lets you show up in local "web design near me" searches and on Google Maps. You'll need your phone, address or service area, and hours. Requires your Google account — do this yourself.

The current favicon is a text SVG placeholder. A proper `.ico` or high-res PNG from your actual logo would look more professional in bookmarks and browser tabs.

The footer has placeholder `#` links. If PocketSod has a Facebook, Instagram, or LinkedIn, those should be wired up. Helps SEO and trust. Give Claude the URLs and it's a 5-minute fix.

## Medium Term (as the business grows)

**8. A real blog post or two**
Even one article like *"5 Things Every Small Business Website Needs"* gives Google something to index beyond your homepage and signals expertise.

Brief pages for DeLara, AZ Powerwash, etc. with "the problem / what we built / the result." These rank for client name searches and serve as sales material.

A standalone `/start` or `/get-a-quote` page linked from ads or social — separate from the homepage contact form, more focused on converting a specific lead.
